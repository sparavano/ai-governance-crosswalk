# ISO/IEC 42001:2023 (Clauses 4–10) ↔ NIST AI RMF 1.0

| ISO/IEC 42001 clause | Closest NIST AI RMF functions / categories | Commentary |
|---|---|---|
| 4. Context of the organization | GOVERN 1; MAP 1 | Internal/external context and interested parties feed the same inputs the AI RMF requires when framing risk: mission, context of use, stakeholders. |
| 5. Leadership | GOVERN 1, 2 | Top-management commitment, AI policy and role assignment map to the RMF's accountability and policy outcomes. |
| 6. Planning | GOVERN 1; MANAGE 1 | AI risk assessment/treatment and AIMS objectives correspond to defining risk tolerance and prioritizing risks. |
| 7. Support | GOVERN 3, 4 | Resources, competence, awareness and documented information support the RMF's workforce and risk-culture outcomes. |
| 8. Operation | MAP 2–5; MEASURE; MANAGE | Operational control of the AI lifecycle, including AI system impact assessment, is where most MAP/MEASURE/MANAGE outcomes are executed. |
| 9. Performance evaluation | MEASURE 1–4 | Monitoring, internal audit and management review parallel the RMF's measurement and tracking of AI risks over time. |
| 10. Improvement | MANAGE 2, 4 | Nonconformity handling and continual improvement align with responding to and recovering from AI risk events. |

## Notes

- Direction of the mapping: 42001 → AI RMF. The reverse mapping differs (some RMF outcomes have no single AIMS home).

- The two instruments differ in nature: ISO/IEC 42001 is a certifiable management-system standard; the AI RMF is voluntary guidance. A mapping indicates support, not equivalence.
